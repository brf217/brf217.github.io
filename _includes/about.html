About Me

I've been a professional data scientist since 2016, with deep experience in finance/investing. I started my career in management consulting, and given that I loved the technical side projects, I eventually started working toward an engineering-based role.

I started programming seriously in 2012 to automate a few repetitive Excel and database tasks, and enjoyed the process so much, I ended up here.

A typical project for me includes everything from gathering requirements, coding source data feeds/ETL pipelines, management of data warehouses, development in BI tools, predictive modeling, and presenting results. I do not rely on a data engineer to wrangle my source data.

My formal education is a B.A. in Economics from New York University and an M.S. in Finance from the University of Colorado. I add to my technical knowledge through online courses or projects requiring new tools.
